## Project Overview

Financial institutions collect large amounts of customer data, but turning that data into actionable insights is critical for effective decision-making. This project explores customer demographics, credit profiles, and behavioral patterns to better understand differences between insurance and non-insurance annuity customers.

The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel and focuses on customer segmentation, financial characteristics, and customer engagement metrics.

---

## Business Problem

The objective of this project was to answer the following business questions:

- What percentage of customers are non-insurance annuity users?
- What are the credit score characteristics of customers?
- How do customer demographics influence customer behavior?
- What customer segments present the greatest opportunities for business growth?
- Are there meaningful differences between insurance and non-insurance customers?

---

## Dataset Overview

The dataset contains customer demographic, financial, and behavioral information, including:

| Variable | Description |
|-----------|------------|
| Credit Score | Customer credit rating |
| Age | Customer age |
| Homeownership Status | Homeowner or Non-Homeowner |
| Geographic Location | Rural, Suburban, or Urban |
| Customer Calling Behavior | Customer engagement frequency |
| Insurance Status | Insurance vs Non-Insurance |
| Savings Balance | Customer savings account balance |

## Key Findings

### Customer Segmentation

- Approximately **65% of customers are non-insurance users**.
- Approximately **35% of customers are insurance users**.
- Non-insurance customers represent the largest customer segment.

### Credit Score Analysis

- Average Credit Score: **667**
- Median Credit Score: **667**
- Approximately **75% of customers have good credit scores**.
- Credit scores are relatively stable across the customer population.

### Customer Demographics

- Average age of non-insurance customers is approximately **48 years old**.
- Homeowners represent a larger portion of non-insurance customers.

### Customer Engagement

- Most customers rarely contact customer service.
- High-frequency customer engagement is limited.

### Geographic Distribution

- Customers are distributed across rural, suburban, and urban areas.
- Urban customers represent the largest geographic segment.

---

## Business Insights

### Insight 1: Strong Non-Insurance Customer Base

The majority of customers belong to the non-insurance segment, indicating opportunities for targeted financial products and customer conversion strategies.

### Insight 2: Financially Stable Customer Population

Customer credit scores indicate a generally strong financial profile, suggesting potential opportunities for additional banking and investment services.

### Insight 3: Low Service Interaction

Most customers demonstrate low customer service interaction, highlighting operational efficiency while presenting opportunities for proactive customer engagement.

### Insight 4: Homeownership Opportunity

The high concentration of homeowners within the non-insurance segment may provide opportunities for cross-selling financial planning, investment, and wealth management products.
